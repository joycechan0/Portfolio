01Who / Why

Where logic learns to dance.

I grew up in Arcadia, drawn early to creative movement — and, just as early, to math and problem-solving. I didn't formally start dancing until freshman year, but the pull toward both the analytical and the expressive has never let up.

That curiosity became coding and data science on one side, and the studio on the other — and I've stopped treating them as separate. Dance taught me to practice something until it's clean; research taught me to question what "clean" even means. I bring both to every problem I take on.

04Reel / Motion · Orchesis

Counting in eights.

I began dancing at fourteen, in Beginning Dance, and found a way to express emotion and connect with music. By sophomore year I'd advanced to Intermediate; now, as a junior in Orchesis Dance Company, I give 20+ hours a week to jazz, hip-hop, and contemporary.

Training built discipline and a deeper sense of self-expression; performing built confidence. The company has grown me not just as a dancer but as a person — on a team that genuinely fosters creativity.

I teach it, too

Choreography and instruction are where dance turns into leadership for me. I'm a paid choreographer for the Foothills Middle School Drill Team, building routines that placed first at multiple competitions — running lesson planning and rehearsal structure on my own. I also serve as an academic mentor in the AHS dance department (a step above a typical TA, 5 hrs/week) and teach private youth classes 6 hrs/week, where students I coached earned spots on selective cheer teams.
